About Shelter

Shelter is a short, quiet game about a little girl named Kao who wakes up alone in a vast green field — with no memory of how she got there.

She doesn't panic. She walks. She looks around. And slowly, gently, she begins to understand the world she's in.

The goal of Shelter is simple: to let the player feel calm. To see something beautiful. To move through a world that feels safe, even when it's unfamiliar. No combat, no pressure — just exploration, soft interaction, and a story that unfolds at its own pace.

Built in Ren'Py 8.5.0 as a 2D side-scrolling experience. Every sound, every step, every moment is designed to carry meaning — and to leave the player feeling something soft.

From the
development diary

February 2026

After a long and difficult journey, we finally made the decision to bring this game to life. We had very little support and resources along the way — but we gave everything we had. This is our first official game, and we hope it carries the warmth and beauty we always imagined it would.

EnginePhysics
February 2020

With more experience behind us, we settled on the name Shelter — and the concept began to take real shape. We wanted every character, every environment, every small detail to carry meaning. We felt ready to make the game. But somehow, life had other plans.

AudioSystems
May 2018

We renamed the project to Lost Memory, as the story had begun shifting away from conflict. We wanted it to be about feeling — about emotion over action. Shelter still existed as a name in the back of our minds, but it didn't feel quite right yet.

WorldArt
Sept 2017

It started with a conversation. We talked about making our very first game together and called it Broken World — an RPG, ambitious and rough around the edges. That name was the beginning of everything.
